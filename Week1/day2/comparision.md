## Task: Write a supportive email to a colleague who just shared that they have been diagnosed with cancer.

## Prompt 1: Basic
Prompt: "Write an email to a coworker who has cancer."

## Output:

Subject: Thinking of you

Dear [Name],

I was so sorry to hear about your cancer diagnosis. Please know that I am thinking of you during this difficult time. If you need anything at all, please let me know.

Wishing you a speedy recovery.

Best,
[Your Name]

## Prompt 2: Improved
Prompt: "Write a supportive and warm email to a colleague who just told the team they were diagnosed with cancer. Wish them well and tell them we are here to support them."

## Output:

Subject: Sending you our warmth and support

Hi [Name],

I was deeply saddened to hear the news about your diagnosis today. I am sending you my warmest thoughts and best wishes as you begin your treatment.

Please remember that the entire team is behind you. We are here to support you in any way we can, whether that means helping with your workload or just being here to listen. Take all the time you need to focus on your health.

Thinking of you,
[Your Name]

## Prompt 3: Detailed
Prompt: "Write a compassionate and professional email to my colleague, Michael, who just informed us about his cancer diagnosis. Express genuine sympathy without being overly dramatic or invasive. Reassure him that the team will cover his current IT projects while he is away. Let him know there is no pressure to reply to this email, and close by offering to help with meals or errands if he needs anything outside of work."

## Output:

Subject: Thinking of you, Michael

Hi Michael,

I was so incredibly sorry to hear the news you shared with us today. Please know that everyone on the team is thinking of you and sending you our absolute best.

Your health and well-being are the only priorities right now. I want to reassure you that we have already coordinated to cover all of your current IT projects while you are away, so please do not give work a single thought. Outside of the office, if there is anything you or your family need—whether it’s picking up groceries, dropping off meals, or running errands—we would be glad to handle it for you.

Please do not feel any pressure to reply to this email or keep us updated. Focus entirely on yourself and resting.

Sincerely,
[Your Name]

## Prompt 4: Creative
Prompt: "Draft a message of solidarity to a coworker facing a serious health battle. Focus on the theme of a supportive community—reminding them that they are a valued part of the team, that their health comes first, and that they have a whole network of people cheering them on and holding down the fort."

## Output:

Subject: We are standing with you

Dear [Name],

In both work and life, we are a community, and right now, our entire community is standing squarely by your side.

As you face this health battle, we want to remind you that you are an irreplaceable part of this team, but your only job right now is to heal. We are holding down the fort here so that you don't have to look back. Consider us your backup crew, cheering you on through every single step of this journey. You are not walking this path alone.

With deepest solidarity and strength,
[Your Name]

## Prompt 5: With Clear Constraints
Prompt: "Write a supportive email to a coworker diagnosed with cancer. Constraints: Keep it under 100 words, do not give medical advice, do not ask intrusive questions about their treatment plan, and explicitly state that they do not need to reply."

## Output:

Subject: Thinking of you

Hi [Name],

I was so sorry to hear about your recent diagnosis. Please know that I am thinking of you and sending you my heartfelt support during this time. Your health comes first, so please take all the time you need to rest and take care of yourself.

There is absolutely no need to reply to this message. We are all rooting for you.

Best regards,
[Your Name]

## comparision and evaluation:

## 1. Basic & Improved Prompts (Low Utility)
- Analysis: These prompts are non-functional for real-world scenarios due to a lack of critical context.

- Limitation: Forcing the AI to generate a message based purely on generic instructions ("write an email to a coworker") results in a template filled with placeholders like [Name] and [Your Name]. The AI is left guessing the missing variables, making the final output unusable without manual editing.

## 2. Detailed Prompt (Highest Utility & Best Overall)
- Analysis: This prompt delivers the most accurate and high-quality result because it provides comprehensive, explicit parameters.

- Strengths: By defining the exact recipient, the specific project coverage, and the precise tone required, it eliminates AI guesswork. The output aligns perfectly with the user's intent and is immediately ready to send.

## 3. Creative Prompt (High Conceptual Utility)
- Analysis: This style is highly effective under specific conditions, particularly when the user lacks inspiration, wants the AI to inject fresh perspective, or prefers a fast, low-effort path to a well-written message.

- Strengths: It allows the user to delegate the formatting and thematic choices entirely to the AI, bypassing the need to dictate every single detail manually.

## 4. With Clear Constraints Prompt (High Structural Utility)
- Analysis: This approach is essential when the output must adhere to strict operational or structural boundaries.

- Strengths: It forces the AI to comply with rigid guidelines—such as strict character or word counts and explicit omissions—ensuring the content fits defined technical or platform limitations.
